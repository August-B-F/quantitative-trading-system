# Global Supply Chain Pressure Index (GSCPI)

**Citation:** Benigno, G., di Giovanni, J., Groen, J.J., and Noble, A.I. (2022). "The GSCPI: A New Barometer of Global Supply Chain Pressures." NY Fed Staff Report 1017 / SSRN 4114973.

**Core mechanism:** Integrates 27 variables from transportation costs (BDI, Harpex container rates, airfreight costs) and manufacturing PMI supply-side components (delivery times, backlogs, purchased stocks) across 7 major economies. Isolates supply-side pressures from demand-side movements.

**Signal:** When GSCPI is elevated (>1 std dev above zero): supply chain stress is high, disadvantages sectors dependent on complex global supply chains (semiconductors, autos), favors domestically-oriented sectors and commodities. When GSCPI normalizes, reverse.

**Data needed:** GSCPI freely available at newyorkfed.org/research/policy/gscpi, monthly, since 1997.

**Performance:** Strong explanatory power for PPI inflation. Correctly captured 2021-2022 supply chain crisis and resolution.

**Weaknesses:** Paper focuses on inflation forecasting, not equity returns -- sector rotation application is inferred. History only from 1997. May lag actual supply chain resolution.

**Assessment:** **Well-constructed, freely available index** from the NY Fed. Sector rotation application is logical but not directly validated. More toolkit component than tested strategy.

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** Use GSCPI level and direction as supply-chain regime indicator. Elevated and rising: penalize tech/discretionary ETFs, favor energy/materials/utilities. Falling: reverse. Monthly frequency matches perfectly.

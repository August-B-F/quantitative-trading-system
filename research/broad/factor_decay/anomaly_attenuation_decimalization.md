# Anomaly Attenuation Post-Decimalization

**Citation:** Chordia, T., Subrahmanyam, A., and Tong, Q. (2014). "Have Capital Market Anomalies Attenuated in the Recent Era of High Liquidity and Trading Activity?" *Journal of Accounting and Economics*, 58(1), 41-58.

**Core mechanism:** The majority of stock market anomalies have approximately halved in magnitude after decimalization (2001). The decline is driven by lower trading costs enabling more arbitrage, growth of hedge fund AUM, increased short-selling, and higher aggregate turnover.

**Signal:** Meta-research informing strategy design:
- Anomalies most sensitive to liquidity improvements decay fastest
- Long-only anomalies in liquid instruments (ETF momentum) face the most arbitrage pressure
- Combining multiple signals is more defensible than relying on any single one

**Data needed:** N/A (meta-research).

**Performance:** Comprehensive study of prominent anomalies pre- and post-2001.

**Weaknesses:** US equities only. Post-decimalization era includes 2008 crisis which may conflate effects.

**Assessment:** Important companion to McLean & Pontiff. Adds the mechanism (liquidity + hedge fund growth) to the observation (returns decline). **Together they provide a complete picture of alpha decay.**

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** Your ETF universe is extremely liquid -- exactly where anomalies attenuate fastest. Argues for combining multiple signals, adding complexity premium, and using realistic Sharpe expectations (50%+ haircut from published literature).

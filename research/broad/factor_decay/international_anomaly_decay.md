# International Anomaly Decay

**Citation:** Jacobs, H. and Muller, S. (2020). "Anomalies across the Globe: Once Public, No Longer Existent?" *Journal of Financial Economics*, 135(1), 213-230.

**Core mechanism:** In the US, anomalies decay ~62% post-publication. But in 38 non-US markets, post-publication decay is mostly insignificant. US-centric arbitrage capital destroys US anomalies but does not efficiently reach international markets.

**Signal:** Momentum signals on non-US asset classes (international equity, EM, commodities) may be less arbitraged and more persistent than US equity momentum.

**Data needed:** N/A (meta-research).

**Performance:** 241 anomalies across 39 markets. US 62%/66% decline; international markets: mostly zero significant decline.

**Weaknesses:** Study is at individual stock level within each country. ETF rotation across countries may differ.

**Assessment:** Genuine insight providing data-driven reason to tilt rotation toward international ETFs. **Real and actionable.**

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** If ETFs include international options (EFA, EEM), trust momentum signals on international ETFs more than US ETFs. Could implement as a "freshness weight" -- slight boost to international momentum scores.

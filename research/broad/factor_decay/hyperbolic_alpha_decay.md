# Hyperbolic Alpha Decay Model

**Citation:** Anonymous (2025). "Not All Factors Crowd Equally: Modeling, Measuring, and Trading on Alpha Decay." arXiv:2512.11913.

**Core mechanism:** Alpha in mechanical (easily replicable) factors decays hyperbolically: alpha(t) = K / (1 + lambda*t). Mechanical factors like momentum fit this model well (R-squared = 0.65). Judgment-based factors (value, quality) do not exhibit the same crowding-driven decay. Post-2015, crowding has accelerated, correlated with factor ETF AUM growth (rho = -0.63).

**Signal:** Classify signals as "mechanical" (pure price-based) vs. "judgment-based" (multi-indicator composites). For mechanical signals, apply hyperbolic decay function. Crowded reversal factors show 1.7-1.8x higher crash probability. Crowded momentum shows lower crash risk (0.38x).

**Data needed:** Factor ETF AUM data, factor return series.

**Performance:** Hyperbolic model outperforms linear and exponential decay models. Tested on US equity factors 1963-2024.

**Weaknesses:** Preprint, not yet peer-reviewed. Game-theoretic derivation has strong assumptions.

**Assessment:** Plausible and useful framework. Directional insight -- simple mechanical signals decay faster than complex ones -- is robust and actionable. **Take specific numbers with caution.**

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** Prioritize composite, multi-input signals over simple single-lookback momentum. Systematically downweight purely mechanical signals (simple SMA, basic TSMOM) in favor of signals requiring multi-step computation. Validates your multi-layered approach.

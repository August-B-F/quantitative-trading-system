# 52-Week High Anchoring

**Citation:** George, T.J. and Hwang, C.Y. (2004). "The 52-Week High and Momentum Investing." *Journal of Finance*, 59(5), 2145-2176.

**Core mechanism:** Investors use the 52-week high as a psychological anchor. When good news pushes a stock near its 52-week high, traders are reluctant to bid higher (anchoring bias), causing underreaction. Nearness to the 52-week high predicts future returns better than past returns alone.

**Signal:** Nearness = Current Price / 52-Week High Price. Sort assets by nearness. Long those closest to 52-week high. Unlike standard momentum, the 52-week-high strategy profits do NOT reverse in the long run.

**Data needed:** Current price and 52-week high. Available for any ETF.

**Performance:** Nearness to 52-week high dominates past returns in predicting future returns. No long-run reversal. Tested 1963-2001. 1800+ citations.

**Weaknesses:** At ETF level, the 52-week high is trivially observable and may already be exploited. For only 8 ETFs, cross-sectional power is limited.

**Assessment:** Real mechanism, heavily cited. ETF-level nearness is correlated with 12M return but more stable and less crash-prone. **Genuine improvement over raw momentum.**

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** Replace or blend standard 12M return momentum with 52-week-high nearness ratio. More robust to outlier months, does not reverse long-term, less susceptible to momentum crashes. Simple: rank ETFs by Price/52WeekHigh instead of trailing return.

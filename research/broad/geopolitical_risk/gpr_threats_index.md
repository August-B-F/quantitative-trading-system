# Geopolitical Risk (GPR) Threats Index

**Citation:** Caldara, D. and Iacoviello, M. (2022). "Measuring Geopolitical Risk." *American Economic Review*, 112(4), 1194-1225.

**Core mechanism:** A text-based index of geopolitical risk constructed from newspaper article frequencies capturing threats and acts of war, terrorism, and interstate tensions. GPR shocks reduce investment, lower equity prices, and increase uncertainty. Decomposes into Geopolitical Threats (GPRT, predictive) and Geopolitical Acts (GPRA, contemporaneous).

**Signal:** Monitor monthly GPRT. When GPRT rises >1 std dev above trailing mean: reduce equity exposure, overweight energy/commodities (supply disruption beneficiaries) and defense, underweight discretionary and financials. GPR increases associated with positive oil returns but negative equity returns.

**Data needed:** GPR index freely available at matteoiacoviello.com/gpr.htm, updated monthly, history from 1900. Country-specific indices also available.

**Performance:** Higher GPR foreshadows lower investment, stock prices, and employment. Published in AER. 3000+ citations. 120+ year backtest.

**Weaknesses:** GPR spikes are relatively rare -- signal is "usually off." Sector implications not directly tested (must be inferred). For monthly rotation, changes may be slow-moving.

**Assessment:** **Legitimate, well-constructed risk measure** with real mechanism. Usefulness for monthly ETF rotation is limited to tail-risk overlay (~5-10% of months). But those are the months that matter most for drawdown control.

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** Use GPRT as conditional defensive filter. When GPRT spikes, increase commodities/energy allocation, reduce financials/discretionary. Ignore when stable. Adds value primarily in crisis periods.

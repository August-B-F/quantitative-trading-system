# Market Intraday Momentum

**Citation:** Gao, L., Han, Y., Li, S.Z., and Zhou, G. (2018). "Market Intraday Momentum." *Journal of Financial Economics*, 129, 394-414. SSRN:2440866.

**Core mechanism:** First half-hour return (previous close to 30 min after open) predicts last half-hour return on the same day for S&P 500 ETF. Driven by late-informed traders and portfolio rebalancers acting near close.

**Signal:** First half-hour return predicts last half-hour return. Strongest on high-volatility days, high-volume days, recession days, and macro news release days.

**Data needed:** Intraday 30-minute price bars (or open, close, and 30-min price). 400+ citations.

**Performance:** Statistically and economically significant across S&P 500 ETF and 10 other actively traded ETFs.

**Weaknesses:** Fundamentally an intraday pattern -- does not directly predict monthly returns. Value for monthly rotation is as a rebalancing execution tool.

**Assessment:** Real mechanism, well-cited. **For monthly rotation, value is in rebalancing execution timing (5-15 bps savings), not portfolio selection.**

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** On rebalancing day, use first-half-hour signal to time entries into last half hour. Over 12 rebalances per year on 8 ETFs, this compounds.

# Sector Rotation via Credit Relative Value

**Citation:** Klein, D. (2013/2021). "Equity Sector Rotation via Credit Relative Value." SSRN 3882113. Winner, 2013 NAAIM Wagner Award.

**Core mechanism:** High-yield credit spreads contain forward-looking information about sector-level equity risk/return not fully priced into equities. Comparing sector-level credit spread signals against equity prices identifies cheap sectors (credit improving faster than equity prices) and expensive ones.

**Signal:** For each sector, compare signal from sector-level HY credit spreads against equity valuation. Rank sectors by credit-implied relative value. Use aggregate HY credit index for tactical allocation (wide spreads = go to bonds). Monthly rebalancing.

**Data needed:** Sector-level high-yield credit indices (ICE BofA sector HY OAS), sector equity ETF prices.

**Performance:** Won Wagner Award ($10,000 prize). Outperformed standard sector momentum and buy-and-hold.

**Weaknesses:** Full methodology is partially proprietary (Capital Context). Sector-level HY data can be expensive. HY credit market smaller before 2000.

**Assessment:** Mechanism is sound (credit leads equity at turning points -- well-established via Merton's structural model). **Likely real alpha, especially at inflection points.** Novel vs. all listed signals.

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** Use sector-level HY credit spread changes as a sector selection overlay. Sectors where credit is improving (spreads tightening) relative to peers get a boost; sectors where credit is deteriorating get penalized. Captures information from a completely different market.

# Industry Lead-Lag Effect

**Citation:** Hong, H., Torous, W., and Valkanov, R. (2007). "Do Industries Lead Stock Markets?" *Journal of Financial Economics*, 83(2), 367-396.

**Core mechanism:** Information diffuses gradually across asset markets due to limited investor attention. Certain "leading" industries -- petroleum, metals, retail, commercial real estate, financial services -- contain predictive information for the broad market incorporated with a 1-2 month lag.

**Signal:** Track monthly returns of 14 leading industries. When leading industries (especially petroleum, metals, retail) have strong positive/negative returns in month t, expect the broad market to follow in months t+1 and t+2. An industry's ability to lead correlates with its propensity to forecast economic indicators.

**Data needed:** Industry-level return data (Kenneth French Data Library, free). Or use sector ETFs directly.

**Performance:** Statistically significant in-sample (1946-2002) and out-of-sample. Confirmed across 8 non-US markets. 1400+ citations. Published in JFE.

**Weaknesses:** Lead-lag effect has likely weakened since publication as more algorithmic traders exploit it. 1-2 month horizon is tight for monthly rebalancing. Predicts market, not individual sectors.

**Assessment:** **Real phenomenon with solid theoretical grounding.** Magnitude has likely decayed post-2007. Core insight (industry returns contain leading business cycle info) remains valid.

**Application to 8-ETF rotation:** Track returns of "leading" sectors (energy, materials, financials) as 1-month forward indicator for risk-on/risk-off tilting. If energy and materials are rallying, suggests improving industrial activity.

# The Crisis Alpha of Managed Futures: Myth or Reality?

**HIGH PRIORITY**

- **Authors:** Raheel Asif, Michael Froemmel, Alexander Mende
- **Year:** 2022
- **Source:** International Review of Financial Analysis, Volume 80

## Core Method

Systematic crisis identification methodology applied to daily CTA returns and sector positions.

## Key Finding

Crisis alpha is REALITY, not myth. CTAs generate positive returns during crises via:
1. Diversification across markets counterbalancing crisis-sector losses
2. Rapid risk reduction — CTAs reduce exposure to crisis markets by 50% in approximately 15 days

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

Implement rapid position reduction rules when an ETF's sector enters crisis (e.g., 50% reduction over 15 days). Use cross-sector diversification as primary defense.

## Related: Kaminski (2014, 2025)

- Greyserman & Kaminski "Trend Following with Managed Futures" documents 800+ years of trend following evidence
- Kaminski (2025): Best protection comes AFTER the initial punch — trend following needs sustained moves. Not effective for sudden 1-day crashes but excellent for prolonged drawdowns.

## Known Failure Modes

- Flash crashes too fast for trend systems to react
- Whipsaw in sideways markets
- Crisis alpha degrades if too many CTAs follow same signals

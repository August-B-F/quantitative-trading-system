# Why Value, Quality, and Momentum Belong Together

**HIGH PRIORITY**

- **Authors:** Noah Beck, Que Nguyen
- **Year:** 2026
- **Source:** Research Affiliates

## Core Method

Integrated signal approach: evaluate securities holistically across Value + Quality + Momentum simultaneously (not stacking separate portfolios).

## Key Findings

1. Value anchors to economic reality
2. Quality filters fragility
3. Momentum adds market confirmation
4. High-quality value stocks had superior returns, lower vol, elevated Sharpe
5. Low-momentum value quintile = highest volatility of all positions

## Performance

High-quality value + momentum: lower drawdowns in 2008, higher returns, lower vol vs. single-factor approaches (1990-2025).

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

Don't use momentum alone. Score ETFs on:
- **Relative value** (P/E, yield)
- **Quality** (earnings stability)
- **Momentum** (12-month return)

Integrated scoring prevents chasing expensive, fragile momentum.

## Known Failure Modes

- Integration methodology can be complex
- Signals may conflict during regime transitions
- Rebalancing frequency trade-offs

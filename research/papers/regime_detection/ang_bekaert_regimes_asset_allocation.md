# How Do Regimes Affect Asset Allocation?

**HIGH PRIORITY**

- **Authors:** Andrew Ang, Geert Bekaert
- **Year:** 2002/2004
- **Source:** NBER Working Paper 10080 / Financial Analysts Journal
- **Links:** [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=467548)

## Core Method

Bivariate regime-switching model for stock and bond returns. Multiple specifications: (I) Basic RS, (II) RS with regime-dependent transition probabilities, (III) RS-ARCH with conditional volatility.

## Regimes

2 states (bull/bear). Bear markets coincide with high volatility, high correlations, and relatively high interest rates.

## Key Findings

- For all-equity portfolios, regime-switching strategy dominates static strategies out-of-sample
- Substantial value added when investor chooses between cash, bonds, and equity
- Investor switches primarily to cash when persistent bear market occurs
- Large market timing benefits because bear regimes coincide with high interest rates

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

DIRECTLY APPLICABLE. Use regime-switching model to determine when to rotate from equity ETFs to bond ETFs or cash. Validates a defensive rotation approach.

## Known Failure Modes

- Regime identification in real-time is noisy
- LR test for regime-dependent transition probabilities not significant (p=0.995)

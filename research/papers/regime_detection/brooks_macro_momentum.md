# A Half Century of Macro Momentum

**HIGH PRIORITY**

- **Authors:** Jordan Brooks (AQR Capital Management)
- **Year:** 2017
- **Source:** AQR White Paper
- **Links:** [AQR](https://www.aqr.com/Insights/Research/White-Papers/A-Half-Century-of-Macro-Momentum)

## Core Method

Systematic global macro strategy using trend-following on macroeconomic fundamentals. Four themes:
1. **Business Cycle:** Changes in real GDP growth forecasts
2. **International Trade:** Spot FX movements against export-weighted baskets
3. **Monetary Policy:** Front-end yield curve variations
4. **Risk Sentiment:** Equity market excess returns

## Data

1970-2016 (nearly 50 years). Assets: Stocks, currencies, long-term government bonds, short-term bonds across many countries.

## Reported Performance

- CAGR: 13% (vs S&P 500: 8.41%)
- Positive Sharpe ratio in EVERY simulated decade
- Low to negative correlation with US equity and bond markets
- In 10 worst equity quarters since 1970 (equities avg -19.9%), macro momentum returned +13.7% excess of cash

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

Use macro momentum signals (GDP forecasts, yield curve moves, FX trends) as regime indicators. When macro momentum signals deterioration, rotate toward defensive ETFs (bonds, gold). When signals improve, shift to equity/growth ETFs.

## Known Failure Modes

- Requires access to cross-country macro data
- Long lookback needed
- May underperform at regime transition points

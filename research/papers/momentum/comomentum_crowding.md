# Comomentum: Inferring Arbitrage Activity from Return Correlations

- **Authors:** Dong Lou, Christopher Polk
- **Year:** 2021
- **Source:** Review of Financial Studies, Vol. 35, pp. 3272-3302

## Core Signal / Method

"Comomentum" measures how much momentum winner stocks comove with each other beyond what fundamentals predict. High comomentum = crowded momentum trade = elevated crash risk. When comomentum is high, momentum transitions from a profitable under-reaction strategy to a risky, crash-prone bet. Momentum lacks a fundamental anchor telling arbitrageurs when to stop, making it uniquely susceptible to crowding.

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

Monitor correlation among your top-ranked ETFs. If the correlation among winners is abnormally high (all equity ETFs moving in lockstep), the momentum trade may be crowded. Consider reducing concentration or adding diversifiers (TLT, GLD).

## Known Failure Modes

- Hard to measure precisely at ETF level
- May generate false signals

## Related: Crowding and Tail Risk (Barroso, Edelen, Karehnke 2022)

Surprising finding: empirically, crowding measured by institutional ownership is associated with LOWER crash risk, not higher. Crowding at the ETF level (very liquid instruments) is likely less concerning than at the stock level.

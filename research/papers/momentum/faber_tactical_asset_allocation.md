# A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation

**HIGH PRIORITY**

- **Authors:** Mebane T. Faber
- **Year:** 2006 (updated 2013, revisited 2016)
- **Source:** The Journal of Wealth Management / SSRN
- **Links:** [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=962461), [Quantpedia](https://quantpedia.com/strategies/asset-class-trend-following)

## Core Signal / Method

10-month simple moving average (SMA) timing rule. Buy when monthly price > 10-month SMA; sell to cash when monthly price < 10-month SMA. Applied independently to each asset class.

## Data Used

US stocks, foreign stocks (EAFE), bonds, REITs, commodities. Tested 1973-2012; extended tests back to 1900.

## Reported Performance (1973-2008)

- CAGR: 11.27%
- Sharpe Ratio: 1.06
- Volatility: 6.87%
- Max Drawdown: -9.6% (vs. -46% buy-and-hold for equities)
- "Equity-like returns with bond-like volatility"

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

DIRECTLY applicable. Apply the 10-month SMA rule to each of 8 ETFs independently. When ETF is below its 10-month SMA, rotate that allocation to cash or short-term bonds. Simplest and most robust trend overlay for any rotation system.

## Known Failure Modes

- Whipsaw losses in trendless/choppy markets (e.g., 2015-2016, 2018)
- Late entry after sharp V-shaped recoveries (e.g., March 2020)
- Transaction costs in taxable accounts
- Performance degrades with daily (vs. monthly) signals

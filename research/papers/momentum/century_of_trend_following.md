# A Century of Evidence on Trend-Following Investing

**HIGH PRIORITY**

- **Authors:** Brian K. Hurst, Yao Hua Ooi, Lasse Heje Pedersen (AQR Capital Management)
- **Year:** 2017
- **Source:** Journal of Portfolio Management, Fall 2017
- **Links:** [AQR](https://www.aqr.com/Insights/Research/Journal-Article/A-Century-of-Evidence-on-Trend-Following-Investing), [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2993026)

## Core Signal / Method

Time-series momentum: go long assets with recent positive returns, short those with recent negative returns. Strategy constructed back to 1880 across 67 markets using 1-month to 12-month lookback windows.

## Asset Classes Tested

Equity index futures, fixed income futures, commodity futures, currency forwards.

## Reported Performance

- Positive average returns in every decade from 1880 to 2016
- Low correlation to traditional asset classes (equities, bonds)
- Performed well in 8 out of 10 largest crisis periods (largest drawdowns for 60/40 portfolio)
- Consistent across recessions, booms, war/peace, high/low interest rates, high/low inflation

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

Directly applicable. Use 12-month time-series momentum as a filter: only hold ETFs with positive trailing returns, move to cash/bonds otherwise. This is the foundational academic justification for trend-following rotation.

## Known Failure Modes

- Trend reversals (whipsaws) in choppy, range-bound markets
- Performance weakest in "quiet" markets without strong trends
- Partially reverses at horizons beyond 12 months

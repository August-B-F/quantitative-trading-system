# Sector Rotation Across the Business Cycle / The Myth of Business Cycle Sector Rotation

**CAUTIONARY — NEGATIVE RESULT**

## Paper 1: Sector Rotation Across the Business Cycle
- **Authors:** Ben Jacobsen, Jeffrey Scott Stangl, Nuttawat Visaltanachoti
- **Year:** 2008
- **Source:** SSRN

## Paper 2: The Myth of Business Cycle Sector Rotation
- **Authors:** Alexander Molchanov et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Source:** International Journal of Finance & Economics (Wiley)

## Core Finding

Even with PERFECT FORESIGHT and zero transaction costs, sector rotation based on business cycle phases generates at best 2.3% annual outperformance since 1948. In realistic settings, outperformance quickly dissipates. Molchanov (2024) independently confirms that historical sector-cycle relationships are unstable and not exploitable in real-time.

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

**IMPORTANT NEGATIVE RESULT.** Do not build the system primarily around business cycle phase identification. Use momentum/trend signals as primary, with macro indicators as secondary confirmation only. Markets price in cycle turns before they are identifiable.

## Known Failure Modes

- The entire premise of business-cycle sector rotation may be a "myth"
- Sector-cycle relationships are unstable over time
- Real-time regime identification always lags

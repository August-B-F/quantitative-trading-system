# The Growth and Inflation Sector Timing Model

**HIGH PRIORITY**

- **Authors:** David Varadi (CSS Analytics)
- **Year:** 2025
- **Source:** CSS Analytics Blog

## Core Signal / Method

Two-indicator regime model:
1. **Expected Growth:** S&P 500 vs. its 200-day SMA (above = rising growth)
2. **Expected Inflation:** Ratio of inflation-positive sectors (Energy, Industrials, Financials, Materials) to inflation-negative sectors (Utilities, Healthcare, Consumer Staples) vs. 200-day median

### Regime-to-Sector Mapping:
| Regime | Growth | Inflation | Favored Sector |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Goldilocks | Rising | Falling | Technology |
| Reflation | Rising | Rising | Energy |
| Stagflation | Falling | Rising | Healthcare |
| Deflation | Falling | Falling | Consumer Staples |

## Data Used

Backtested from 1990.

## Reported Performance

Substantially higher returns than S&P 500 with comparable volatility and reduced max drawdown.

## Application to 8-ETF Rotation System

HIGHLY applicable. This 2x2 regime framework can directly inform ETF selection. Map each of 8 ETFs to a regime, then overweight ETFs matching the current regime. Uses only price-based indicators (no macro data lag).

## Known Failure Modes

- Binary regime classification may miss transitions
- Single-sector concentration
- Assumes stable sector-regime relationships
